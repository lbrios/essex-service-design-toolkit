    A financial assessment tells us how much you can afford to pay towards your care and support.

    It looks at your:

    • Income – such as pensions and benefits
    • Capital – such as savings, investments, property and land

    We will not look at the income or property of your partner in the financial assessment.

    Complete a financial assessment

    We will contact you about a financial assessment if we agree you have care and support needs we should meet under the Care Act 2014.

    After we have contacted you, you can: complete your financial assessment online.

    If you don’t want to complete a financial assessment or tell us your financial circumstances, we will assume you can pay for the full cost of your care and we will charge you that amount.

    Contact us if you have any questions about your financial assessment.

    What happens after your financial assessment?

    We will send you a letter that explains how much you need to pay towards your care and support and how we worked this out.

    See our examples of what a financial assessment outcome looks like and what it means:

    • Care at home: your financial assessment outcome explained (66 KB PDF)
    • Residential care: your financial assessment outcome explained (69 KB PDF)

    When you receive your financial assessment outcome, please check that all the information is correct and let us know straight away if something is wrong.

    What help is there for self-funders?

    Even if you pay for your own care and support, you can still benefit from contacting us.

    As a self-funder you are still entitled to have an assessment of care needs. We will look at your specific needs and identify services which can help you.
