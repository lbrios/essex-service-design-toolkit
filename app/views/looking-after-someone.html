    • Looking after someone

    A 'carer' is someone who - without being paid - regularly looks after, helps or supports someone who wouldn't be able to manage everyday life without their help.

    You may be caring for a partner, relative, friend or neighbour.

    Help from Carers First

    The first point of contact for carers in Essex who need some extra help is Carers First.

    Call 0300 303 1555 or fill in the Carers First contact form and someone from their team will aim to contact you within 5 working days.

    Caring for an adult

    Support for people caring for a partner, relative, friend or neighbour over the age of 18

    Parent carers of disabled children

    Get help if you look after a child with disabilities, a long-term health condition, or learning difficulties

    Help for young carers

    Support for people under 18 who look after someone who is ill or disabled, or uses drugs or alcohol

    Carer's rights in the workplace

    If you are a working carer, you have rights to paid and unpaid time off to care for people who depend on you

    Financial support for carers

    See what financial help is available to you as a carer

    Emotional support for carers

    Get support if you are feeling exhausted, overwhelmed, or stressed
