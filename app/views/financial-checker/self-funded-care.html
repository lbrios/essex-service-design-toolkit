What help is there for self-funders?

Even if you pay for your own care and support, you can still benefit from contacting us.

The process of paying for care can be complicated and every case is different.

We also recommend you seek independent financial advice

Check your benefits entitlement

Care and support you don’t have to pay for

Some care and support is free, including:

  • Short term support and rehabilitation for people who have been in hospital (lasts up to 6 weeks)
  • NHS continuing healthcare which is arranged and funded solely by the NHS
  • Aids and minor adaptations costing £1,000 or less
  • Support services offered to carers - for more information contact Carers First

What happens next?

As a self-funder you are still entitled to have a care needs assessment. We will look at your specific needs and identify services that can help you.

If you have already been assessed as needing care, you will be offered a financial assessment so we can check if you are eligible for financial support.

You may also want to know about:

  • Social activities and support groups
  • Free advice and guidance from local providers
  • Deferred Payment Scheme for residential home charges
