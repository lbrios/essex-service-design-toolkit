Financial assessment

What is a financial assessment?

A financial assessment tells us how much you can afford to pay towards your care and support.

It looks at your:

  • Income – such as pensions and benefits
  • Capital – such as savings, investments, property and land

We will not look at the income or property of your partner in the financial assessment.

How do I get a financial assessment?

If we agree you have care and support needs we should meet under the Care Act 2014, we will contact you to arrange a financial assessment.

You can choose to complete the financial assessment form:

  • yourself or ask someone to do it on your behalf
  • by telephone with a member of the financial assessment team (not applicable in all cases)
  • by a face to face visit with a member of the financial assessment team

If you choose to complete the form yourself, we recommend you seek independent benefits and financial advice.

Find out what you need to tell us in a financial assessment.

If you don’t want to complete a financial assessment or tell us your financial circumstances, we will assume you can pay for the full cost of your care and we will charge you that amount.

Contact us if you have any questions about your financial assessment.

What is a personal budget?

Personal budget is the amount of money we have worked out you need to pay for your care and support needs.

How is it used?

There are 3 ways you can take a personal budget, they are:

  • we will spend your personal budget and arrange your care and support
  • you will arrange your own care and support using a direct payment
  • a combination of both us arranging your care and support, and you arranging your own care and support

Your social worker will go through this with you when you agree your care and support plan.
