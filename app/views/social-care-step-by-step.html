    Contact Adult Social Care for an informal chat about your care and support needs.

    A social worker or occupational therapist might visit you to discuss your care and support needs and advise you on the next steps.

    If you are assessed as needing care and support, a social worker will agree a care and support plan with you. The plan gives details of:

    • how your support will be provided
    • how much it costs
    • who will be paying for it

    Some people are entitled to financial support towards the cost of care. This depends on your financial circumstances.

    Use our 5-minute financial checker to find out if you, or the person you are looking after, might be eligible for financial support.

    Complete your full financial assessment so that we can work out how much you will need to pay towards your care.

    If we have agreed to pay for some or all of your care, we will give you a personal budget. You can use this to:

    • arrange your own care and support
    • authorise the council to arrange care and support on your behalf
    • arrange some care and support yourself, and have some arranged for you

    This will be disccussed when your social worker creates your care and support plan.

    Your social worker will review your needs on a regular basis to make sure you are getting the right level of care and support.

    Tell us if your care needs have changed

    We will review what you pay towards your care each year. This is called an annual reassessment. The reassessment accounts for any changes to benefits and legislation that all local authorities must follow. It will also explain any changes to care charges.

    It's important to tell us as soon as possible if there are any changes to your circumstances. f you don't, this might have an impact on your annual reassessment.
